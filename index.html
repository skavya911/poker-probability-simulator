01
What is Poker
Poker is a probability-based decision game where each player acts under incomplete information. Unlike games of pure chance, poker rewards players who understand mathematical odds, read their opponents, and manage risk. Every action — call, raise, fold — carries an expected value. The goal isn't just to hold the best hand; it's to make the best decision given what you know at each moment.
02
Rules
Each player receives 2 private hole cards. Five community cards are revealed in stages. The player who forms the best 5-card hand — from any combination of their 2 hole cards and the 5 community cards — wins the pot. You may use both, one, or neither of your hole cards.

♣ About this project
Poker Probability
Simulator

This project demonstrates how probability is not fixed — it evolves with information. Using Monte Carlo simulation, the system generates thousands of possible outcomes at each stage of a poker game to estimate real-time winning chances.

What makes poker a powerful analytical model is uncertainty. A single card can completely reverse the expected outcome — a dominant hand can suddenly lose, while an underdog can emerge victorious in one moment.

This simulator visualizes decision-making under incomplete information, showing how probabilities dynamically shift across the game.

A probabilistic system where outcomes are not predicted — but simulated.

Game Dynamics — How Outcomes Flip
Instant Flip
You hold trip Queens, but an opponent completes an inside straight on the river, reversing the outcome instantly.
Close Fight
Both players make four-of-a-kind from the board, where the smallest difference determines the winner.
Domination
Pocket Aces versus a weak starting hand demonstrates near-certain probability before community cards appear.
Underdog Wins
Even the weakest starting hand can win, showing how low-probability events still occur.
